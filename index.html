Fear versus facts

Nuclear power is one of the safest energy sources we have available, over 350 times safer than coal, yet people still fear it. When most people think of nuclear energy they think of Chernobyl style melt downs or glowing green goo in yellow barrels with skulls on it, but that's far from the truth. Media portrayal has distorted our vision of one of mankind's greatest creations, a sustainable renewable amazing energy source.

But why does this matter, whether the people fear nuclear power or not? The reason is because we live in a democracy where people's thoughts and perceptions drive changes. For example, in California in 1976, a law was passed in which no new nuclear power plants could be installed in the state, this is still in effect today, driven by the public's lack of knowledge on the subject.

In this project we will examine 5 nuclear incidents that happened over the course of the years, talk about what went wrong, and how these incidents won't happen again due to modern engineering and extremely advanced safety systems.
